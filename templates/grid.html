Hi translator and welcome to Polish, a user-friendly translation tool!


Basic flow

1. In the top menu there is a list of languages. Click on the one that you would like to translate sentences to.

2. What you see now is a list of the untranslated sentences for the language you selected. On the left side you can find the keys in the default language. On the right side you can see the translation for each key in the left. The right side is the same as the left side because these sentences are not translated.

3. To translate a sentence, click above its text in the right side and a text area will appear.

4. Translate the text to your language and then click Save translation. If you do not want to save the translation, you can click Cancel. If you think that the translation of this sentence is the same as in the default language, click Mark always as translated and the system will consider this sentence as translated forever.

5. When you click the Save translation button, the translation is saved on your session, but it is not yet part of the final po file. To achieve that, you need to click Generate po file in the top menu. A list of all the changes that you have done will be showed and you can re-edit them if you need to. Once you are confident with your translations you can click the Write button to export your translations into the po file.



Search

Polish also allows you to search for words or parts of words. You can click Search in the top menu a simple form will appear. You can select to search within the translated or untranslated keys, or both of them. Moreover, you can also decide if you want to search within the key or the translation or both. Then you need to click the Search button and a list of the matching entries will appear.



Status

Polish allows multiple translators to translate sentences in the same language without them having to care about not translating the same sentence. You can see who is currently translating by clicking Status in the top menu. You will also be shown some extra information about the language you are translating such as total amount of keys, total amount of untranslated keys, who is currently translating and how many sentences they have translated but not exported.



That's it! Pretty easy right? You can now start using Polish and translate sentences in a cool and easy way! :-)
